  - Colonial documents and correspondence
  - Oral history recordings
  - Photographic collections

- **University of the West Indies Library** (St. Augustine Campus)
  - West Indiana and Special Collections
  - Folklore Archive
  - Caribbean periodicals

- **National Library and Information System Authority** (NALIS)
  - Trinidad and Tobago heritage collections
  - Digital archives
  - Rare books collection

- **Schomburg Center for Research in Black Culture** (New York)
  - Caribbean diaspora materials
  - Folklore collections
  - Historical documents

### Field Research Opportunities

Those interested in conducting original research on Papa Bois traditions should consider:

1. **Northern Range Communities**: Villages bordering forest reserves where hunting traditions remain active
2. **Tobago Forest Areas**: Main Ridge Forest Reserve and surrounding communities
3. **Forestry Division**: Officials and rangers with extensive field experience
4. **Hunter Communities**: Traditional hunters who maintain Papa Bois beliefs
5. **Environmental Education Programs**: Schools and NGOs using Papa Bois in curriculum
6. **Cultural Festivals**: Carnival, Heritage Festivals where Papa Bois appears

### Ethical Research Considerations

Researchers should:
- Obtain proper permissions from university ethics boards
- Respect cultural ownership of traditional knowledge
- Provide appropriate compensation to informants
- Return research findings to communities
- Avoid exploitation or appropriation
- Recognize spiritual significance to practitioners
- Protect sacred sites and sensitive information
